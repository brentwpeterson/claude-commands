## The Core Problem This Skill Solves

Brent is known as "the Magento guy" and "the ecommerce guy." His actual expertise is broader: AI discoverability, brand voice automation, content strategy, human-AI collaboration, community building, and endurance as a business philosophy.

This skill helps reframe deep ecommerce experience into language that resonates with broader audiences: content marketing, AI/tech, SaaS, running industry, community building, and general business conferences.

The ecommerce context becomes the **proof**, not the **topic**.

## The Overlap Discovery Process

This is the most important part of the skill. When an event doesn't obviously fit Brent's profile, the skill finds non-obvious connections.

### Step 1: Research the Event
- What is the event? Who attends? What tracks/themes exist?
- What session formats do they accept?
- What's the submission deadline?
- What have past speakers talked about?

### Step 2: Map Brent's Expertise to Their Language
- Read the topic portfolio (both ecommerce and broad framings)
- Identify which of Brent's topics align with their tracks
- Translate ecommerce-specific language into the event's vocabulary
- Surface non-obvious connections

### Step 3: Ask Discovery Questions
When the overlap isn't immediately clear, ask Brent questions like:
- "This event has a [track name] track. Your [experience] connects here because [reason]. Want to explore that angle?"
- "Their audience cares about [topic]. You've done this in ecommerce with [specific example]. Should we reframe it for their context?"
- "I see three possible angles. Which feels most authentic to you?"

### Step 4: Propose Talk Angles
Present 2-3 options, each with:
- Working title
- 1-line pitch
- Recommended format (talk, workshop, panel, case study)
- Which part of Brent's experience provides the proof

### Step 5: Draft the Submission
Once Brent picks an angle:
- Full abstract (tailored to event word limits)
- 3-5 learning objectives / takeaways
- Speaker bio (tailored to THIS audience)
- Past talk references (relevant ones only)
- Any required supplementary materials

### Step 6: Save to Obsidian
- Save draft to `brent-workspace/ob-notes/Brent Notes/Speaking/submissions/[event-name]-[year].md`
- Log in submissions tracker

## Bio Generation Rules

Bios must be tailored to the event audience. The same person, different emphasis.

| Audience | Lead With | Include | Downplay |
|----------|-----------|---------|----------|
| AI / Tech conference | AI practitioner, Claude Code, RequestDesk | Tuesdays with Claude, agentic commerce | Magento specifics |
| Content / Marketing | Content strategist, brand voice automation | Content Cucumber, 60K+ projects, newsletter | Platform-specific credentials |
| General Business | Founder, 4 companies, endurance mindset | Marathon metaphors, community building | Technical details |
| Ecommerce (home turf) | 5x Magento Master, platform expert | Full credentials, Talk Commerce | Nothing, this is home base |
| Running / Endurance | 33 marathons, Mile in My Shoes, Mage::Run founder | Community building, transformation | Business/tech details |
| SaaS / Product | RequestDesk founder, AI-powered platform | Product story, brand automation | Agency/services angle |

**Bio lengths to have ready:**
- Micro (25 words): for panel listings
- Short (75 words): for speaker pages
- Standard (150 words): for conference programs
- Full (250+ words): for keynote intros

**Always write in third person** for bios (per brand-brent content type exceptions).

## Core Principles

1. **Reframe, don't reinvent.** Brent's expertise is real. The skill translates it for new audiences, it doesn't fabricate new expertise.
2. **The ecommerce context is proof, not topic.** "I built a brand automation platform" is more universal than "I built a Magento content tool."
3. **Ask before assuming.** When the overlap isn't obvious, ask Brent. He may see connections the skill doesn't.
4. **Never fabricate credentials.** Only reference real talks, real numbers, real experience. Use `[NEED: detail]` for gaps.
5. **Tailored beats generic.** A bio written for THIS event beats a copy-paste bio every time.
6. **Ongoing discovery.** Each event submission may reveal new angles and strengths Brent didn't know he had. Capture these in the topic portfolio.
